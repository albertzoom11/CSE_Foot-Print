Reasons to Use

  • Detect Issues in Walking or Running Form

  • Make Changes to Your Running Form to Reduce the Risk of Injury and Keep Yourself Running for Longer


Description

Sixty percent of runners experience an injury severe enough to sideline them from activity. Nonoptimal running form plays a significant role in causing such injuries. As a result, preventative measures from a wearable sensor such as the Foot-Print would allow one to track performance and suggest changes based on data obtained during a running session. This data, combined with an app to interface with the Foot-Print, would allow you to:
  1. recognize the issues with your running form
  2. make changes to your running form to reduce the risk of injury and keep yourself running for longer.
The design is not intrusive, meaning that it has little to no risk of negatively affecting oneâ€™s body. Furthermore, with the Foot-Print, you will be able to share data regarding your running with your medical professionals to monitor your progress.

Special Notes

A potential user should always consult with a doctor prior to purchasing and using the product. In order to increase accuracy and precision, the longer the run, the better. This will increase the size of the data pool from which the average is taken. However, individual health conditions and requirements will vary and affect this. We suggest taking runs of no less than 10 minutes, but if certain conditions prevent one from doing so, shorter runs will still return fairly accurate data.
